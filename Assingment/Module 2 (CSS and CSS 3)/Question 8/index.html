Question 8 : What is the use of the background-position property?

The background-position property in CSS specifies the position of a background image within its containing element. It allows you to control the horizontal and vertical positioning of the background image.

Here are some possible values for background-position:

top left - the top left corner of the image is positioned at the top left corner of the element (default value).

top center - the top center of the image is positioned at the top center of the element.

top right - the top right corner of the image is positioned at the top right corner of the element.

center left - the center left of the image is positioned at the center left of the element.

center center - the center of the image is positioned at the center of the element.

center right - the center right of the image is positioned at the center right of the element.

bottom left - the bottom left corner of the image is positioned at the bottom left corner of the element.

bottom center - the bottom center of the image is positioned at the bottom center of the element.

bottom right - the bottom right corner of the image is positioned at the bottom right corner of the element.
